Time

What is it, and what does it do?

 

Time: the human perception of universal motion (the motion of the universe and all that is in it), and the motion of the individual perceiving it relative to the greater whole. Or more simply put, time is the human perception of universal relativity.

 

Time is motion, motion is time. So what does time do? It moves…

 

Every measure of time that humans have is based on motion. A day is one revolution around the sun. An hour is 1/24 of the previously defined day, which is just motion. A quartz clock uses the vibration of a small sliver of quartz, that is the moving back and forth of a small rock, to provide a frequency that is measured to define time. The very notion of frequency, is motion relative to some other motion. The nanoseconds, or picoseconds of a computer clock are just fractions of the unit seconds, which are in turn fractions of the unit minutes, which are in turn fractions of the unit hours, which as we know are fractions of the unit day, which is simply the motion of our planet around the sun. Time is nothing but motion, or more accurately, the motion of multiple bodies moving relative to each other. It is the differences between the motion of two or more objects, that enable the very concept of time in our perception.

 

Much like with frequency, time can only be defined relative to something else. With frequency, we have a number of repeating events, that happen relative to another repeating event. Frequency, that is clock ticks per second, is the number of repeating clock ticks that happen, every time a second is repeated. Seconds are the number of clock ticks (units) that happen every time an hour is repeated, and so on. Each definition relies on another definition, and each other definition is either based on motion, or is a fraction (or a multiple) of a unit based on motion. Whether we’re measuring the motion of planets, oscillating rocks, or atoms, its all about motion.

 

Time and motion are opposite ends of a spectrum, two sides of a coin. Much like yin and yang, light and dark. Relative motion creates and describes the perception of time, time creates and describes the perception of relative motion, one cannot exist without the other. Much like the Golden Eyed Commander of Wishes, and the Old Man of Wandering Mountain, neither are the cause of the others actions, but are infinitely intertwined by their very existence. They depend on, and support one another.


Dimension: A plane of existence in which a whole element or concept, is described by the relationships of two or more elements that make up the whole. For instance, in a single dimension of space we can define points, and a number of points greater than one, and their relationships relative to each other in the dimension, will define a line.


Putting together three dimensions of space expands our points and lines, out to points, lines, and faces. These three dimensions describe our physical world, or at least they would if the world was static. These three dimensions cannot describe motion. When we add motion to the idea of existence, we have to add another dimension...time. The dimension we call time, quite literally exists to describe motion, that is its entire purpose. Time describes motion, motion describes time.
